Bucharest's tech market is healthier than its reputation suggests — but it has rules of its own. Here's what I tell candidates moving into Romania.

## The market is bigger than fintech

Most foreigners arrive thinking the only options are UiPath, Bitdefender, and a couple of consultancies. The reality includes a healthy SaaS scene, a growing AI/ML cluster, and significant nearshoring outposts for EU and US firms. Salaries in Bucharest are lower than Berlin or Amsterdam but cost of living catches most of that gap.

## What recruiters look for

- **Demonstrated impact** in your past three roles, ideally in numbers
- **Fluent English** (Romanian helps, but is not required for most roles)
- **EU work authorization or a clean blue-card story**
- A LinkedIn that matches your CV — recruiters check both

## Where to look

Linkedin is the dominant channel, followed by ejobs.ro and the company-direct route. Reach out to engineering managers directly with a short pitch — most respond.

## Three things that move the needle

1. A portfolio link that shows real production work
2. An open-source contribution or technical blog post in the last six months
3. Two or three Romanian engineers willing to vouch for you

If you do those three before sending a single CV, you'll outperform most candidates with stronger paper credentials.
